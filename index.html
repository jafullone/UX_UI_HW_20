Background

Using the design thinking process, our team worked to design a mobile application to solve a common problem faced. Three person teams were created, where we assigned responsibilities modeling today's workplace. We conducted research, prototyped and iterated on our designs, and presented to our peers.

Team: Jonathan I., Rich S., Lainey A., Jennifer F.

My Role: My responsibilities conducting market research, ideating solutions, included prototyping UI, usability testing, and presenting this solution.

The Challenge

Research, prototype, test, and validate an idea for a brand-new mobile application that solves a real, clearly identified need in usersâ€™ everyday lives.

The Solution

Providing college students a secure way to report an item as either lost or found, keep record of your important items, and provide students a safe way to retrieve their items.

Defining the Problem

User Research

To get an understanding of the depth of this issue we reached out to students who have lost or found something while on a college campus. All interviewed students attended universities where no such technology existed; thus, for our competitor analysis we sought apps that served communties who had lost or found something.

Participants

We conducted six user interviews with college students who live on campus.

Methodology

We conducted six remote user interviews with college students who live on campus. We wanted to understand the pain points when losing an item, finding a lost item, and the role of saftey.

Competitor Analysis

We looked at what might be direct and indirect compeitors of Find & Seek. We analyzed the way in which accounts were utilized as well as who each competitor serves.

Understanding the Users

We observed that students:

  1. Had more urgency to find an item if it was perceived as valuable
  2. Noted several ways methods used to find a lost item
  3. Felt mostly hopeless that their lost items may not be returned and unsure about how to find or return a lost item

Problem Statement

On-campus college students are frustrated that they finding lost items isn't as simple as looking in the lost in found. The current process has proven stressful for students, who usually end up having to spend time going to each place hoping to retrieve lost items when urgently needed.

Students need a more effective system of communicating lost items to the student body/campus to alleviate stress and a safe avenue of retrieval of items when found.

Analyzing the Research

Based on the research gathered from the interviews, we discovered there were six basic themes that users experienced:

  1. Items of value and urgency
  2. Current return/retrieval process
  3. Personal information willing to provide
  4. Pain points around losint items
  5. Personal safety practices
  6. Feelings/outcomes about a lost item

Brainstorming Solutions

Using the findings from the competitor analysis along with our user research, we began to brainstorm features to incorporate in our app.

We used dot voting to guide which features to prioritize.

Early Ideation

User Flow

During the user flow process we identified that our services catered two two different individuals: someone who had lost an item and someone who had found one while on campus. This required two different user flows.

Low/Mid-Fidelity Prototype Screens

Using our feature prioritization matrix and user flow as guides, we began sketching our design for the app. Based on what we had gathered, we knew that this prototype was to include: a way for someone who lost an item on campus to be alerted if the item is found; creating a backpack feature wherein students could store items to easily mark them as lost; a safe way for the exchange of an item that has been found.

Usability Testing

Test Plan

We conducted testing with five different subjects.

Objective: To test the prototype for any pain points throughout various points of using the Find and Seek app

Tasks:

  • Go through the process of being a person who finds an item and successfully reports it
  • Go through the process of being a person who loses an item and uses the app without making an account
  • Go through the process of being a person who loses an item who has a My Backpack and report it as missing

Usability Testing Analysis

We found that we needed to focus on being clear with how the buttons are labeled as well as reassess the current user flow and make improvements. We also realized, based on user feedback, that there were considerations to be made regarding the appropriateness of accounts.

User Feedback

  1. Terminology used on buttons were confusing
  2. A little confusing when you lose something without an account, especially with backpack
  3. Some of the back buttons do not work otherwise really great layout

Final Design

After analyzing and prioritizing the user feedback, I iterated over the design and updated the prototype.
